John MacArthur

John MacArthur is a well-known pastor, author, and teacher.

John MacArthur was the pastor of Grace Community Church in Sun Valley, California, since 1969 until 2025. Was also the president of The Master's University and The Master's Seminary. MacArthur was known for his expository preaching style and his commitment to biblical inerrancy. He has authored numerous books on Christian living, theology, and biblical studies. MacArthur was known for his advocacy of expository preaching, and was recognized by Christianity Today as one of the most influential Christian preachers of his era.

John wrote or edited more than 150 books. Among them The MacArthur Study Bible, sold more than one million copies and received a Gold Medallion Book Award. In 2021, he worked with a team from The Master's Seminary and the Lockman Foundation to produce the Legacy Standard Bible (LSB), an update of the 1995 edition of the New American Standard Bible NASB.

  • Born: June 19, 1939 (age 84 years), Los Angeles, California, USA
  • Spouse: Patricia MacArthur (m. 1961)
  • Education: Talbot School of Theology (1963), Bob Jones University
  • Awards: ECPA Christian Book Award for BIBLE (1991), ECPA Christian Book Award for BIBLE (1996)
